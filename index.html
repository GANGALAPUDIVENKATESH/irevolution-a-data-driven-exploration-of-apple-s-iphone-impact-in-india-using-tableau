History

Apple India — Key Milestones

2017
Formal Market Entry
Apple officially establishes its India business. iPhone 7 becomes the first major push, targeting premium segment consumers in metros.
🍎
2017
Market Share: 1.4%
1.36 million units sold. Limited reach, high import duties making iPhones 28%+ more expensive than in the US.
2019
Local Assembly Begins
Wistron begins assembling iPhone XR in Bengaluru. Tax relief follows, reducing consumer prices.
🏭
2019
First Made in India iPhone
iPhone XR becomes the first major model assembled in India. Opens door to price competitiveness in premium segment.
2020
iPhone SE — Budget Breakthrough
iPhone SE 2020 at $399 opens Apple to upper-middle-class India for the first time. 0.72M units in Q3 alone.
💡
2021
PLI Scheme Launched
India's Production Linked Incentive scheme brings Foxconn and Pegatron. Jobs surge from 8K to 22K in one year.
2023
First Apple Stores Open
Flagship stores in Mumbai's BKC and Delhi's Saket open in April 2023. Tim Cook personally inaugurates. Queues span blocks.
🏪
2024
Manufacturing Hub
40% of iPhones sold in India are assembled locally. Exports hit $8.5B. India becomes Apple's second-largest manufacturing hub.
Data Insights

What The Data Reveals

Key findings from the Tableau analysis across all dimensions.

📊

Premium Paradox

Despite high prices, iPhone demand in India surged 731% in 7 years — driven by EMI financing (now 72% of purchases) and status signalling culture.

🇮🇳

Make in India Momentum

PLI-driven local assembly reduced effective iPhone prices by 8–12%, unlocking new buyer segments previously priced out of the ecosystem.

🏙️

Urban-Rural Divide

Top 8 metro cities account for 58% of all iPhone sales. However Tier-2 cities (Pune, Ahmedabad, Jaipur) grew 3× faster in 2023–24.

👥

Youth-Led Demand

The 18–35 age group drives 68% of iPhone purchases in India. Average buyer age fell from 32 in 2017 to 25 in 2024 — the youngest globally.

🔄

Trade-in Ecosystem

iPhone trade-in rates jumped from 28% (2020) to 53% (2024) — India now has one of the most active Apple certified refurbished markets in Asia.

📱

Online-First Nation

70% of iPhone purchases in India happen online (Flipkart, Amazon, Apple.com) — a dramatic shift from 15% in 2017, accelerated by COVID-19.
