Research Methodology

How We Built This

Data sources, collection methods, analysis framework, and limitations of the iRevolution project.

📋 Research Overview

iRevolution is a data analytics project that examines Apple's iPhone impact on the Indian smartphone market from 2017 to 2024. The project combines publicly available market research data, government reports, and Apple's financial disclosures to build a comprehensive picture of India's iPhone revolution.

📊 Data Sources

  • International Data Corporation (IDC) India

    Quarterly smartphone tracker reports — unit shipments, market share by brand

    Primary
  • Counterpoint Research India

    Premium segment analysis, consumer surveys, channel data

    Primary
  • Apple Inc. Annual Reports (10-K)

    Revenue breakdowns, Greater India/APAC segment data

    Official
  • Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY)

    PLI scheme data, manufacturing jobs, export figures

    Official
  • CMIE (Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy)

    GDP per capita by state, income segment classifications

    Secondary
  • TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India)

    Internet penetration, mobile data usage statistics

    Secondary
  • CMR (CyberMedia Research)

    Consumer behavior surveys, satisfaction scores

    Secondary

india_smartphone_market_share.csv

Competitive landscape data covering 6 major brands from 2017–2024. Market share percentages are estimated based on IDC and Counterpoint quarterly reports, cross-referenced for accuracy.

regional_adoption_india.csv

State-level data for 8 major Indian states, covering iPhone user counts, penetration rates, and socioeconomic indicators. State-level iPhone data is estimated using IDC regional splits and census population figures.

consumer_demographics.csv

Synthetic dataset derived from published survey data (CMR, YouGov India) on smartphone buying patterns. Represents purchasing behavior segmented by age, income, and gender.

apple_india_economic_impact.csv

Manufacturing and economic data sourced primarily from MeitY PLI scheme reports, Apple press releases, and India EXIM bank export statistics.

⚠️ Limitations & Caveats

1. Estimated Data: iPhone-specific unit sales in India are not officially disclosed by Apple. All figures are market research estimates with ±15% confidence intervals.

2. Regional Splits: State-level breakdowns are interpolated from regional (North/South/East/West) IDC data using population and GDP weighting.

3. Currency: All USD figures use 2024 average exchange rates (1 USD = ₹83.5) for historical comparison. Actual purchasing power may differ.

4. Market Share: Total smartphone market figures include feature phones in some analyst definitions; premium segment share differs from overall share.

5. Academic Use: This project is for educational and data visualization practice only. Not intended for commercial decision-making.

📏 Analysis Framework

The project follows a structured analysis approach:

1. Trend Analysis: Time-series decomposition of quarterly sales data to identify seasonality, growth rates, and structural breaks (COVID-19, PLI scheme).

2. Geographic Analysis: Choropleth mapping of penetration rates against socioeconomic indicators (GDP per capita, urbanization, internet access).

3. Demographic Segmentation: Cross-tabulation of buyer characteristics to identify primary and emerging customer segments.

4. Economic Attribution: Measurement of Apple's contribution to manufacturing jobs and export revenue using MeitY's PLI reporting framework.

5. Forecasting: Tableau's built-in exponential smoothing model applied to historical sales data for 2025–2027 projections.
